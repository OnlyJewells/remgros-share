    Remgro’s share value is back on track after six years

    The share value of Remgro— Johann Rupert’s investment holding company was on track for its greatest performance in over six years, after notifying the market that it plans to transfer its 28 percent stake in RMB Holdings to investors.

    In the morning trading period, Remgro’s share price rose 5.31 percent to R194.50, having risen as much as 8.2 percent earlier. It’s been on track for the best day since June 2013.

    In a separate official statement, RMB Holdings announced on Tuesday that it would distribute its R130 billion shareholdings in FirstRand to the shareholders. The share price of RMB was 4.53 percent to R86.93, having risen as much as 5.6 percent earlier.

    RMH is essentially a holding structure with a 34% stake and, as a result of changing corporate governance norms and growing investor scrutiny, these forms of holding structures have been abolished, Vestact analysts said in a report.

    For those who don’t know, Johann Rupert is a South African billionaire businessman and the chairman of the Richemont— a Swiss luxury company. He manages a prestigious portfolio of luxury brands, including fashion labels Azzedine Alaïa, Chloé, and Dunhill, as well as renowned jewelry and watches brands such as Van Cleef & Arpels, Vacheron Constantin, and Cartier.

    As president of the Swiss luxury company Richemont, South African billionaire businessman Johann Rupert manages a prestigious portfolio of luxury brands, including fashion labels Azzedine Alaïa, Chloé and Dunhill, as well as renowned jewelry and watch brands such as Van Cleef & Arpels, Vacheron Constantin, and Cartier. The company also bought 100% of its shareholding in the luxury e-tailer Net-a-Porter, a deal worth more than $500 million in 2010.

    In 1988, he established Richemont as a spin-off of Rembrandt Group Limited, a company formed in the early’ 40s by his father Anton Rupert. In 1993 Rupert divided the tobacco and luxury goods activities of the company into two subsidiary companies, with the Vendôme Luxury Company managing the watch, leather goods and clothing operations of the company.
